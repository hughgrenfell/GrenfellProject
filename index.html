History of the caribou logo

In 1907, Wilfred Grenfell, a medical missionary to Newfoundland from London, imported 300 Norwegian reindeer. The reindeer were intended to be used as food and draft animals. Some of the reindeer were sick with a parasitic roundworm that infected the local caribou. All of the imported reindeer eventually perished but the strain of roundworm affects the native caribou species to this day. The caribou logo serves as a reminder of the far reaching effects of our decisions.
